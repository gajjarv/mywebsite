Connecting the Cosmos: GNU Radio Based SETI Curriculum

Connecting the Cosmos is a cutting-edge radio SETI curriculum that teaches the basics of radio astronomy, digital communication, and signal processing. By utilizing the advanced capabilities of GNU radio software and the rich data generated by the SETI Instituteâ€™s Allen Telescope Array, our goal is to prepare the next generation of astronomers and radio communication experts. The curriculum is designed to be engaging and inspiring, with a strong emphasis on hands-on learning and real-world applications. Week 3: Introduction to Radio Astronomy I

This weeks materials aims to introduce students to the fundamentals of radio astronomy, radio telescopes, and basic signal processing techniques. GNU Radio Companion (GRC) files are included to demonstrate important signal processing topics including the Nyquist theorem, Fourier transforms, and modulation/demodulation techniques. The lab has students building an FM radio receiver in GRC, to be integrated with a low-cost RTL-SDR device, which gives students experience with filters, demodulation, and data visualization techniques.
